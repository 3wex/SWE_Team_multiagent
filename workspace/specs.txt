Specifications for Overly Dramatic Command-Line Calculator:

I. Introduction
The calculator will be a command-line application built in Python, designed to perform basic arithmetic operations (addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division) on two numbers.

II. Functional Requirements
1. The calculator will take two numbers and an operation as input from the user.
2. It will then perform the specified operation and display the result.
3. The calculator will express reluctance and dissatisfaction with performing math operations, using dramatic language to convey its 'distaste' for the task.
4. For each operation, the calculator will output a unique, overly dramatic message before and after performing the calculation.

III. Edge Cases
1. Division by zero: The calculator will output a dramatic message expressing its 'horror' at being asked to perform such an 'atrocious' act, and then exit the program.
2. Non-numeric input: The calculator will output a dramatic message expressing its 'disgust' at the user's 'incompetence', and then exit the program.

IV. Expected Behavior
1. Upon launch, the calculator will output a dramatic message expressing its 'dread' of being used for math.
2. After each calculation, the calculator will output a dramatic message expressing its 'relief' that the task is complete, and ask the user if they wish to perform another calculation.
